◈ HOW IMAGE DETECTION WORKS

📸 REAL HUMAN PHOTO

A genuine photo taken by a real person on their phone or camera. Shows real things from the real world — people, places, food, nature, streets, buildings. Has natural camera grain/noise. Real lighting and real shadows. Nothing is "too perfect". The image is unedited and unfiltered.

🤖 AI / FAKE IMAGE

ANY image that was NOT taken by a real camera. This includes: AI-generated images (made by Midjourney, DALL-E, Stable Diffusion, etc.) · Cartoons and animations (drawn or 3D rendered) · Anime style art · Fantasy / unrealistic illustrations · Fake or digitally created scenes that never existed in the real world.

KEY SIGNAL — CAMERA NOISE: Every real photo taken by a phone or camera always has natural sensor grain (noise level 10–70). AI-generated images and cartoons have near-zero noise because they are created by software, not captured by a lens. This is the single most reliable way to tell them apart. Additional signals: color palette complexity, flat region ratio, edge type, gradient smoothness, and texture frequency.
